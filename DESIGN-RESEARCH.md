# Design research: OutsourcedLabor.com

## Search intent

Query: `Philippines outsourced labor staffing company`

The page needs to help an owner who is considering Filipino talent for recurring operations work. It should feel practical and controlled, not like a talent marketplace or a cost-savings ad.

## Five direct competitors reviewed

### 1. Sourcefit

URL: https://sourcefit.com/

Sourcefit uses oversized type, orange accents, facility footage, accordion-style benefit rows, awards, client logos, and a dense set of operating statistics. The scale is convincing, but the inspected page had unusually large blank spaces and an apparently empty orange band. The hero also sends visitors toward several different actions at once.

Useful idea: pair a plain promise with evidence about the operating process. Avoid: cost claims, logo walls, and the sprawling page length.

### 2. Booth

URL: https://hirebooth.com/

Booth has the strongest visual system of the five. Its forest green, mint, lavender, and warm off-white palette feels modern without looking like generic software. The hero uses very large type, real workplace imagery, rounded crops, and compact metric cards. Service and process modules are easy to scan. In the inspected capture, a large report popup covered much of the hero, while some later media appeared not to load.

Useful idea: a confident dark hero, a human focal point, and operational cards that overlap the image. Avoid: popups, invented metrics, and Booth's exact shapes or copy.

### 3. Cloudstaff

URL: https://www.cloudstaff.com/

Cloudstaff uses electric blue, employee portraits, a split hero, role cards, certification claims, and repeated conversion buttons. The people and role examples make the offer easy to understand. The page is long, the CTA labels compete, and the cookie/newsletter layers clutter the first screen. A few large media areas appeared blank in the inspected capture.

Useful idea: show the people and the actual work categories early. Avoid: multiple CTA labels and unsupported retention or savings claims.

### 4. Outsourced

URL: https://outsourced.ph/

Outsourced uses navy and cyan with a hot-pink action color. Its opening combines a direct Philippines proposition with staff illustrations, benefit cards, a four-step process, and broad service-category cards. The page gives visitors plenty of ways to explore roles, though the mix of illustration and photography feels inconsistent and several carousels visibly crop content.

Useful idea: name the Philippines in the proposition and make role choices concrete. Avoid: cost percentages, star ratings, client claims, and a carousel-heavy layout.

### 5. Staff Domain

URL: https://www.staffdomain.com/

Staff Domain uses royal blue, lime accents, bold sans serif type, a subtle topographic pattern, and two large operating-model panels. The headline is sharp and the primary action is clear. The page relies more on awards and abstract patterning than human imagery. The inspected capture also showed very large blank gaps between sections.

Useful idea: frame the offer as an operating system rather than a loose supply of workers. Avoid: product tiers, badge clutter, and empty right-side hero space.

## Selected direction

Winner: Booth's dark, people-first editorial system.

The redesign borrows the underlying logic, not Booth's execution: a forest hero, one strong human focal point, oversized type, compact operating cards, warm secondary colors, and clear section rhythm. The page adds Staff Domain's emphasis on operating controls and Outsourced's direct Philippines language.

## Original implementation

- Built an original "operations bench" concept around queues, approval lines, and review loops.
- Wrote a new H1: `Build a Filipino operations bench without losing the controls.`
- Added the deployment marker `labor-ops-2026-07`.
- Created an original operations-specialist SVG and stored it at `/public/filipino-operations-specialist.svg`. The illustration shows an observable desk scene; nearby copy, rather than the person's appearance, states the Philippines-only sourcing rule.
- Replaced the old monogram with an original task-bench logo made from stacked work rows and a completed check.
- Used forest, warm paper, safety orange, and yellow rather than Booth's full palette.
- Used process detail in place of testimonials, ratings, client logos, awards, price cards, and savings claims.
- Kept the existing service, guide, contact, legal, sitemap, robots, and footer routes.
- Kept the independent referral disclosure visible. Staffing requests may be routed to a partner, and every presented staffing option is limited to talent in the Philippines.

## Image and asset note

Both the hero illustration and logo were drawn for this site as local SVG assets. No competitor assets, screenshots, logos, or copy are used on the page.
